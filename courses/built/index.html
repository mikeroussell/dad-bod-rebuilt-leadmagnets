Why Zebras Don't Get Ulcers
(And You Do)

When a lion chases a zebra, the zebra's stress response kicks in. Heart rate spikes. Energy floods the system. It runs for its life.

But here is the key: Once the zebra escapes, it goes back to grazing. It shakes it off. The stress cycle completes.

You? You never stop running. The email, the traffic, the argument, the bills—your body is stuck in a chronic fight-or-flight response. You never complete the cycle. You just stew in stress hormones.

This is what Robert Sapolsky calls the "Zebra Principle." The biological cost of chronic stress isn't just exhaustion—it's visceral fat, low testosterone, and burnout.

The Biology of Willpower

The biology of stress and the biology of self-control cannot coexist. When stress hormones flood your system, your prefrontal cortex—the logic center—literally goes offline. You don't eat chips because you're weak. You eat them because your brain is in survival mode.

The First Domino System

Everyone has a "first domino"—the one habit that slips before everything else crashes. Whether it's skipping meditation or late-night snacking, we teach you to identify your unique early warning sign and catch the spiral before it starts.
